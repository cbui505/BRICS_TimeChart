BRICS: GDP and Human Development 1970-2050

Out of all the BRICS nations, Russia dominated both GDP and HDI up until the 1980s. However, the collapse of the Soviet Union in 1991 weakened Russia's GDP and HDI growth significantly, while strong rises for the other BRICS countries allowed Brazil, China, and India to catch up in terms of GDP in 1996. Since then, Russia's development in GDP and HDI has been severely lagging behind that of the other BRICS nations, and in the near future, projections indicate that Brazil, China, and South Africa will catch up to Russia's HDI as early on as 2023, while India on track to catching up some time after 2050.
